The best opener style for all flows (not wordy, not story-dump)

Use a two-sentence opener, then stop.

Your best “map” opener (10–15 seconds)

“I’m a hardware test and validation engineer. I’ve owned production test stations end to end, including fixtures and wiring, comms, and automation, and I spend most of my time debugging hardware issues and making tests repeatable.”

Then stop.

If they want details, they’ll ask:

“Tell me about one”

“What kind of comms?”

“What instruments?”

“How big was the station?”

That’s when you bring in Terran.

Why leading with an example can be risky

Some managers want the 30,000-foot view first. If you start with a specific story immediately, you can:

pick the wrong example

trigger irrelevant follow-ups

burn time before they even know your scope

So the fix is: capability first, example second.

Practical rule you can follow every time

Give 2 sentences.

Stop.

Offer the fork:

“Want the quick example from Terran, or the overview of my last couple roles?”

This makes you look controlled and senior.
